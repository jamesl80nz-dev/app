Split Step Reaction Drill

A dynamic movement and reaction drill designed to improve player readiness, explosive first movement, and court positioning.

Objective

Develop efficient split step timing, reaction speed, balance, and explosive first movement toward the ball.

Kit List

  • Tennis balls
  • Cones or markers
  • Tennis court
  • Optional agility ladder
  • Optional stopwatch or scoring board

Set-Up

  • Player starts in central recovery position
  • Coach or feeder stands opposite side of net
  • Cones can be placed wide forehand/backhand as movement targets
  • Players perform split step before reacting to feed or visual cue

How To Run With A Group

  • Split players into pairs or small groups
  • One player works while others feed or observe
  • Rotate every 60–90 seconds
  • Keep intensity high but durations short
  • Focus on quality movement over volume

Variations

  • Reaction to coach pointing left/right
  • Reaction to live fed ball
  • Add volley finish
  • Add recovery movement after each feed
  • Use coloured cones for visual reaction training
  • Add fitness conditioning between repetitions

Gamification Options

  • Points awarded for successful reactions
  • Timed rounds with score leaderboard
  • Team relay competitions
  • Penalty for incorrect first movement
  • Reaction speed challenge using stopwatch timing

Watch-Outs

  • Players jumping too high during split step
  • Flat-footed landing
  • Crossing feet during first movement
  • Over-fatiguing younger players
  • Poor recovery positioning between repetitions

Limitations

  • Can become repetitive without variations
  • Requires strong coaching observation for quality execution
  • Does not fully replicate live match pressure unless gamified
  • Limited tactical decision-making unless expanded into point play

Coaching Cues

  • "Land balanced and ready"
  • "Explode off the outside leg"
  • "Small adjustment steps"
  • "Stay light on your feet"
  • "Recover quickly to neutral"
